About Tulalens

Today, when upper- and middle- income people have a choice to make, they often turn to Yelp, TripAdvisor, and other sites where they can share and retrieve personal experiences and reviews. This kind of crowdsourced information is powerful: it can improve decision-making and accountability.

How can low-income people also benefit from the power of crowdsourced information? That’s the question we asked, and Tulalens is our answer. Tulalens is a system for some of the world’s poorest communities in emerging markets – people without internet access – to share feedback and retrieve crowdsourced information on organizations that provide them with healthcare and other critical needs. We're a social enterprise based in Boston. Tula means balanced in Sanskrit. Our name reflects our aim, which is to balance the lens, or create actionable insights by listening to the needs of low-income communities.

The results so far are powerful. In our pilot, 114 pregnant women in an Indian slum used our system, and the following happened within a month: 25% of women switched to a higher quality prenatal care facility, 43% advocated for better services at their facility, and 100% discussed the data with their husbands.

The opportunity is vast. Many pregnant women we work with believe they can access only one prenatal care facility near their home. Even when they are treated poorly or forced to pay bribes, they continue going to that facility. By sharing crowdsourced data on fees, quality of the provider, wait time, bribes paid and more, women now have critical information that will allow them to make informed decisions on which of several facilities to attend.

It's time to expand our work. Since launching in Oct. 2014, we have reached 600 women in need of reproductive health services across three of the largest slum areas in Hyderabad. In these instances we were collecting and sharing information in person. Now that we've tested the approach, we're ready to build a prototype mobile technology that will allow us to scale.
