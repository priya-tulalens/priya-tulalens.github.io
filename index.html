We're looking for a Design Fellow in Chennai, India.

Design Fellow Job Description:

Overview of Tulalens: Tulalens is a startup social enterprise headquartered in Boston, MA with operations in Chennai, India. We envision a world in which under-served communities are knowledgeable consumers, not passive recipients of products and services. Tulalens currently accomplishes this by collecting and sharing crowdsourced information from and for pregnant women living in the slums of Chennai on the quality of health services in their community. This allows women to make informed decisions on where they seek care.

Summary of Role: The design fellow will use principles of human-centered design to modify and improve upon our service by deeply understanding the needs of our customers. The Design Fellow reports to the Founder/CEO, and will be based in Chennai, India. This is a full-time paid position that will last for six months, from January 2016 to June 2016, with the possibility of extension.

What We’re Really Looking For: We’re looking for people who are fiercely passionate about using design thinking to improve the lives of those in under-served communities. Are you excellent at making sense of seemingly chaotic amounts of information? Do you believe in the power of information to transform the lives of under-served communities? Do you get excited about how to best present complex data in a simple, digestible form? If yes, we’re looking for you.

Responsibilities:

  • Strategize and prioritize with Founder/CEO on the assumptions we want to test, and the most effective ways to test these assumptions in keeping with human-centered design principles
  • Work with our implementing team in India to quickly test different aspects of our service
  • Recommend refinements based on learnings from design thinking processes and work with India team to implement them

Skills and Background:

  • You’ve previously applied design thinking skills to refine products and/or services used by under-served communities
  • You have a deep sense of empathy and are a strong listener and observer
  • You’re willing to adapt, learn and improve quickly
  • You’re passionate about designing the way that data is collected and communicated to improve lives
  • You’re fluent in both English and Tamil
  • Bonus: You have experience working at the intersection of health, data presentation and design

Contact Us: If this role sounds like the ideal position for you, please send your resume, one to two samples of your work related to human-centered design, and a brief paragraph on what excites you about this role to priya@tulalens.org.
